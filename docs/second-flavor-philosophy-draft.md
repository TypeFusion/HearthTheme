# Second Flavor Philosophy Draft

Updated: 2026-04-05

Working codename: `Moss`

## Core Statement

`Moss` is a yellow-green-led color language for code interfaces.

It is designed for users who want faster structure recognition, clearer syntax landmarks, and a more energetic editing mood than `HearthCode`, while still keeping the restraint needed for everyday work.

## Core Thesis

Moss should feel:

- awake but not frantic
- vivid but not neon
- sharp but not brittle
- disciplined but more animated than Hearth

The theme should not feel like a novelty object.
It should feel like a serious working environment with stronger visual signals.

## Product Relationship

HearthCode Ember and Moss should not compete as near duplicates.

They should form two clearly different product choices:

- `HearthCode`: warm, steady, long-session, low-glare
- `Moss`: paper-and-pigment, dry, vivid, yellow-green-led

The difference should be visible immediately, not only after careful comparison.

## User Promise

Moss promises:

- faster syntax landmarks
- more immediate structure recognition
- clearer separation at key semantic roles
- stronger momentum in active coding sessions

It is the flavor for users who want their editor to speak sooner.

## Surface Philosophy

Moss should not derive its identity from a tinted background.

Its surfaces should:

- stay mostly neutral
- stay controlled
- support contrast
- get out of the way of syntax landmarks

The substrate is there to frame action, not to perform personality on its own.

## Role Philosophy

Colors are still assigned by meaning before widget.

But unlike Ember, Moss should intentionally push the most important landmarks farther apart.

- `keyword` should feel immediate
- `function` should be one of the clearest anchors on screen
- `type` should stand in a visibly separate lane
- `string` should support fast scanning, not only long-form reading rhythm
- `comment` should remain quiet enough to keep the page stable

This flavor should win through semantic jump, not through universal loudness.

## Preferred Chroma Direction

The most promising direction for this flavor is yellow-green-led, not olive-led.

That means:

- yellow and yellow-green can carry more of the product signature
- cyan-blue should remain the cool structural counterweight
- coral or orange-red should act as pressure and interruption accents

This should feel closer to signal lighting than to natural foliage.

The product should not read as "green theme".
It should read as a sharper, more energized code language built around citrus, chartreuse, and cool-electric balance.

## Energy Model

Moss should use concentrated pigment, not ambient intensity.

That means:

- local peaks are stronger
- large neutral regions still exist
- emphasis is intentional
- saturation is selective

The theme should feel more active than Hearth because the important things are easier to notice, not because everything is more colorful.

## Climate Philosophy

The four climates should remain, but their character must stay consistent with the product.

- `dark`: flagship expression, strongest identity, most direct signal
- `darkSoft`: calmer night version, still more energetic than Hearth soft
- `light`: bright studio clarity with firm syntax separation
- `lightSoft`: approachable and reduced, but never sleepy

Soft climates should still feel like the same product.
They must not collapse back into Hearth's quieter energy.

## Anti-Goals

Moss should avoid becoming:

- cyberpunk theater
- retro-effect nostalgia
- gamer-neon spectacle
- a cold corporate blue pack
- a minor variation of Hearth

It should stay:

- professional
- intentional
- clearly differentiated

## Public Positioning Draft

Moss is the pigmented line in the system:

- for faster scanning
- stronger landmarks
- sharper structure
- and a more energized coding mood

Where HearthCode Ember warms the page, Moss dries it.
