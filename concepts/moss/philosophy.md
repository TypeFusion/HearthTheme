# Moss Philosophy

Moss is a paper-and-pigment color philosophy for code interfaces.

It treats color as a semantic language before it becomes a platform theme.
The goal is not just to look different from `HearthCode Ember`.
The goal is to create a distinctly different color atmosphere:

- drier materials
- clearer pigment lanes
- lighter paper character
- the same level of design discipline

## Core Thesis

Moss should feel:

- dry but not brittle
- pigmented but not muddy
- airy but not washed out
- distinctive but still daily-drivable

If HearthCode Ember is tuned around warmth and ember glow, Moss should tune the page around paper, pigment, and yellow-green rhythm.

## Vocabulary

The palette speaks through a smaller but more forceful set of named families:

- `carbon`: neutral substrate and structural frame
- `chalk`: readable carrier neutrals
- `spark`: hot interruption and control-flow pressure
- `voltage`: cool callable anchor and structural lighthouse
- `citron`: yellow-green signal family for visible classification
- `jade`: living support structure for properties and literals
- `amber`: measured numeric heat and secondary warmth

## Role Philosophy

Colors are assigned by meaning before widget.

- `keyword` should feel immediate
- `function` should anchor the page quickly
- `type` should stand in a clearly separate lane
- `string` should support fast scanning, not only quiet reading rhythm
- `comment` should remain disciplined enough to preserve page stability

This flavor wins through semantic jump, not through making every role equally loud.

## Surface Philosophy

Surfaces should remain mostly neutral.

Moss should not derive its identity from a tinted background.
Its substrate should frame pigment and preserve clarity, while the syntax landmarks carry most of the product voice.

## Variant Philosophy

The four variants should share one personality:

- `dark`: flagship identity, strongest signal
- `darkSoft`: calmer but still distinctly alive
- `light`: bright and clean with firm structure recognition
- `lightSoft`: reduced and approachable, but never sleepy

Soft variants must still feel lighter and drier than Ember's corresponding climates.

## Product Principle

Moss should be a real second product choice, not an alternate material mood.

It should be the line users choose when they want:

- paper-and-pigment materials
- yellow-green-led rhythm
- a drier page with the same design discipline
- a clearly different color taste from Ember

The difference from Ember should be visible immediately, not only explainable after the fact.
